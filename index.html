A trade-data record of rare-earth proxies and other critical materials—built from frozen USITC DataWeb workbooks, with China-reporter UN Comtrade used only where the series is genuinely comparable and USGS Data Series 140 kept as national physical context.

Read the denominator. The U.S. workbooks contain 18 selected partners, not World. Every U.S. “share” on this page says so. Trade origin is an access signal—not mine ownership, reserves, or proof of state intent.

05 · Method & provenance

Every claim resolves to a frozen source row

The pipeline preserves source quantity buckets, suppression counts, period type, workbook name, and row identifiers before calculating any share or concentration index.

Reporter

United States

USITC DataWeb imports use country of origin; domestic exports use ultimate destination. Exports to China are not treated as China’s total access.

Periods

Annual + comparable YTD

Full-year charts end in 2025. All 2026 annual source zeros are labeled unavailable. Jan–Apr YTD is its own 1993–2026 series.

Quantities

No false joins

Value and first quantity match exactly by source unit bucket. Second quantities remain independent rows because HTS4 does not expose a lossless join.

Continuity

Breaks stay visible

HTS revision years are review checkpoints. A documented 8505 quantity-regime break is marked at 2019. Missing and suppressed quantities remain labeled.

Frozen inputs

Frozen statistical inputs

Two USITC DataWeb workbooks, 32 frozen China-reporter UN Comtrade responses, and one USGS Data Series 140 workbook.
