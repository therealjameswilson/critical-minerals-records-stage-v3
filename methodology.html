Method & provenance

How the statistical record is built

The analytical boundary is simple: no claim outruns its reporter, product scope, denominator, period, or source vintage.

01 · Evidentiary model

Statistics first; FRUS de-emphasized

V3 covers 1993–2026 and does not use FRUS as evidence. Its partner-level U.S. layer comes from frozen USITC DataWeb workbooks. Its China sourcing layer comes from frozen China-reporter UN Comtrade API responses. Frozen USGS Data Series 140, Mineral Commodity Summaries 2026, and Minerals Yearbook 2022 files supply national and mine-production context.

Trade statistics are treated as access signals. They do not directly measure reserves, mine ownership, beneficial ownership, processing capacity, control of third-country assets, or policy intent.

02 · USITC DataWeb

The U.S. reporter view

Two workbooks, three measures per period, and one deliberately narrow denominator.

Imports

Country of origin

Imports for consumption use customs value. Origin is not assumed to be mine origin or ownership.

Exports

Ultimate destination

Domestic exports use FAS value. Exports to China are never substituted for China’s total imports.

Scope

18 selected partners

The source has no World row. U.S. China shares use China divided by the selected-partner sum.

Products

25 HTS4 headings

Four-digit categories are traded-product proxies; several are chemically or technologically broad.

Period treatment

Full-year data end in 2025. Every 2026 cell on the annual sheets is a structural zero while the YTD sheets contain January–April trade. The ETL replaces annual 2026 with an explicit unavailable state and publishes January–April as a separate comparable 1993–2026 series.

Quantity treatment

Value and first quantity join exactly by flow, period, partner, heading, description, and normalized source quantity bucket. Second quantities do not expose a lossless key to a first-unit value bucket at HTS4. They remain independent rows; they are never copied across value buckets.

Numeric DataWeb suppression indicators are preserved as counts. A positive count marks quantity incomplete and prevents average-unit-value calculation for that bucket.

03 · USGS Data Series 140

National rare-earth history

Rare-earth-oxide-equivalent context for 1900–2020; displayed on the site for 1993–2020.

The repository freezes the USGS rare-earth workbook and normalizes U.S. production, imports, exports, apparent consumption, current and constant-1998-dollar unit values, and world production. Quantities are metric tons of rare-earth-oxide equivalent unless the source identifies a unit-value measure.

Source NA and W cells remain explicit unavailable and withheld states. The six worksheet formulas are retained verbatim with their cached values. Method labels follow the embedded notes at the series and year-range level; production cells remain generically qualified because the source does not label each as reported or estimated. USGS identifies the source and usage as public domain.

This national series has no partner or HTS dimension. It is kept separate from—and never feeds—the DataWeb China-share, supplier-HHI, or HTS unit-value calculations.

04 · USGS current publications

MCS 2026 and MYB 2022

Current commodity observations and country mine-production context, kept separate from reporter-specific trade.

The repository freezes the official MCS 2026 ScienceBase commodity CSV and XML metadata, the current Rare Earths, Heavy Rare Earths, Scandium, and Yttrium PDFs, and the MCS version history. The Windows-1252 (cp1252) CSV yields 286 observations for the four exact chapter labels. The observations cover 2021–2025: 2026 is the publication vintage, not an observation year.

The current MCS release is version 1.3, reposted 27 May 2026, but the frozen ScienceBase CSV predates changes in its Rare Earths PDF. Version 1.3 changes Brazil 2025 reserves from 21 million to 11 million metric tons and the 2025 world reserve lower bound from more than 85 million to more than 75 million metric tons. Version 1.1 moved footnote 14 away from China 2024 production and attached it to India 2025 reserves, where a 2015 OSCOM report gave 256,000 tons of monazite reserves but no rare-earth reserve figure. The current view marks China’s raw quota note as superseded and keeps India reserves unavailable with the new context attached. Original and current fields remain side by side, and each action is listed in the revision-audit CSV.

The MYB 2022 advance-table workbook remains frozen in full, but only table T8 is normalized: 65 source-row/year world-mine-production observations—12 countries plus the source total across 2018–2022. A combined display leaves 2023 explicitly missing before MCS observations resume in 2024; no interpolation is used.

Mine production identifies reported extraction, not ownership, refining, control, or guaranteed access. MCS import-source shares are direct or shipping sources and may differ from mine origin. No USGS publication row enters the DataWeb China-share, supplier-HHI, or HTS unit-value calculations.

USGS identifies these U.S. Government releases as public domain. The repository retains their attribution and revision vintage; its MIT License applies to original site code and documentation.

05 · UN Comtrade

The China reporter view

All reported origins for annual China imports of HS 2846, 1993–2024.

The downloader makes one public-preview request per year for China-reporter imports of rare-earth compounds, with the primary partner interpreted as origin. Each raw JSON response is frozen byte-for-byte with its request URL, retrieval timestamp, classification code, publication metadata, and SHA-256 digest.

Acquisition fails if the API reports an error, omits the requested period, returns 500 rows, or does not reconcile the sum of non-World partner values to the World record. All 32 years reconcile exactly in this vintage.

China has no comparable annual 2025–2026 record in the frozen Comtrade series. The site does not interpolate those years. It marks HS revision bands and the change from China’s Special to General trade system in 2000.

The defensible finding is that new origins became major reported sources. Trade values alone do not prove that the PRC cultivated those sources through state action or investment.

06 · Proxy boundaries

Rare earths are not one clean tariff line

HTS 2846

Rare-earth compounds

The cleanest four-digit heading used in this build; also covers yttrium and scandium compounds.

HTS 2805

Broad metals heading

Includes products beyond rare-earth metals, so it is a proxy rather than a chemically pure series.

HTS 8505

Broad magnet heading

Includes non-rare-earth magnets, electromagnets, holding devices, couplings, brakes, and parts.

General HS revision years—1996, 2002, 2007, 2012, 2017, and 2022—are shown as continuity-review checkpoints, not assumed product breaks. HTS 8505 has a source-evident quantity-regime break in 2019, when value shifts sharply from “no units collected” into kilogram-denominated buckets.

07 · Reproduction

Frozen inputs, deterministic outputs

python3 -m venv .venv
. .venv/bin/activate
python -m pip install -r requirements.txt
python scripts/build_trade_data.py
python scripts/validate_data.py
python -m pytest -q

Validation independently recomputes China shares and supplier HHI, checks audited headline values, enforces annual/YTD separation, verifies quantity-slot and suppression rules, validates every Comtrade digest and World reconciliation, checks the USGS row contracts, revision audit, explicit 2023 gap, and source isolation, and keeps browser payloads within their size budgets.
