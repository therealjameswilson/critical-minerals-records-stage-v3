Evidence and comparison rules

Methodology for a statistics-only V3

V3 treats access as a set of source-defined indicator lanes. It preserves where a statistic was measured, who reported it, what period and unit it covers, and what it cannot prove.

01 · Scope

1993–2026 is a new evidence regime

V2 is a FRUS-led historical portal covering 1861–1992. V3 begins in 1993 and does not use FRUS documents, diplomatic narrative, archive leads, or model-generated prose as evidence. The only live claims in V3 come from official U.S. Government statistical source files retained by the project.

Core rule: if the source does not support a value or relationship, the interface shows a gap. It does not generate a placeholder statistic.

The words “U.S. access” and “PRC access” therefore label a framework, not a precomputed score. Trade, mine production, processing capacity, reserves, recycling, consumption, and stockpiles remain distinct.

02 · Current source layer

USITC DataWeb release 01

The first release freezes two workbooks exported from USITC DataWeb on July 10, 2026. DataWeb republishes official U.S. merchandise-trade statistics originating with the Census Bureau.

  • Imports: imports for consumption, customs-value basis, reported country of origin.
  • Exports: domestic exports, F.A.S.-value basis, reported destination.
  • Query scope: 18 selected partners and 25 four-digit HTS traded-product categories.
  • Years: 1993 through 2026, with both mixed annual/current-YTD and matched January–April tables.

The workbooks are committed unchanged. Their query-parameter worksheets, byte sizes, access dates, and SHA-256 hashes are retained in the public source register.

03 · Time

Partial periods do not masquerade as full years

DataWeb supplies a main value sheet with full annual values through 2025 and January–April 2026, plus a year-to-date sheet containing January–April for every year. The explorer defaults to the latter.

Chart break: in the mixed view, V3 breaks the line before 2026 and labels the year as January–April YTD. It does not connect a full 2025 directly to a partial 2026 as one continuous annual series.

Future sources must preserve observation year separately from edition or publication year. A “2026” publication may report 2025 production; V3 will not infer a 2026 observation from the cover date. Revisions create a new source vintage instead of overwriting the earlier file.

04 · Geography

Every country appears in a statistical role

A country name alone is insufficient. V3 records whether a geography is the reporter, importer, exporter, reported origin, destination, production location, reserve location, processing location, asset location, or owner/controller.

China and Hong Kong remain separate source-reported statistical/customs areas. Macao and Taiwan would also remain separate if later sources include them. A combined geography would require a visible project derivation with dated membership.

05 · Product scope

HTS4 is a trade category, not a mine

The selected four-digit headings include named ores and materials, broad chemical baskets, mixed metal groups, and manufactured products. V3 labels these scopes in the selector. For example, HTS 2846 is a named rare-earth-compound category, while HTS 8112 combines several minor metals and HTS 8505 includes permanent magnets, electromagnets, and parts.

A product code cannot by itself establish the source deposit, mineral content, owner, processing path, final use, or strategic importance. Classification changes and mapping breaks will create separate series segments rather than being smoothed away.

06 · Measurement

Zero, blank, suppressed, and absent are different

The normalizer retains literal zero values and suppression flags. A missing cell stays null. A partner/category combination absent from the export is shown as “not reported in export,” not zero.

Value sheets may divide one HTS4 category into several rows because the underlying products use different quantity descriptions. V3 sums the dollar values within the same flow, partner, HTS4 category, and period. It does not sum physical quantities across incompatible units.

  • Imports keep the source's customs-value basis.
  • Domestic exports keep the source's F.A.S.-value basis.
  • Both are nominal dollars; V3 does not present them as constant-dollar trends.

07 · Comparison

Only like measures share a chart

A full U.S.–PRC comparison will require multiple source families. Before two observations can be overlaid or used in arithmetic, they must share a declared comparison group covering the same metric, material form and scope, supply-chain stage, period, frequency, quantity or valuation basis, normalized unit, and geography role.

Prohibited examples

  • China mine production versus U.S. import value.
  • Gross product weight versus rare-earth-oxide-equivalent content.
  • Reserves versus resources.
  • Annual values versus unmatched year-to-date values.
  • Country of origin versus ownership or control.
  • Imports for consumption versus general imports without a method note.

08 · Derivation

Calculated values must be reproducible

Future shares, concentration measures, or ratios will be marked as project-derived. They must expose the formula, version, source observation IDs, missing-value rule, denominator rule, and rounding rule. Missing inputs propagate null.

Prohibited output: V3 does not calculate an “access score,” “strategic advantage score,” or weighted “who is winning” index that blends unlike statistical lanes.

09 · Verification

The public build is reproducible from frozen files

The repository's ingestion script reads the value sheets, validates the exact 1993–2026 query period and trade-flow labels, normalizes compact browser series, and writes the dataset registry. The validator then checks:

  • year boundaries, periods, geography roles, value bases, and array lengths;
  • distinct China and Hong Kong identities;
  • source-file presence and SHA-256 hashes;
  • source-manifest and normalized-series counts;
  • visible public caveats and comparison status;
  • absence of V2 FRUS-era data artifacts.

Continuous integration rebuilds the JSON and fails if it differs from the committed output.
