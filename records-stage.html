Trust architecture

How to read FRUS and this portal

FRUS is indispensable, but it is selected and edited rather than a complete archive. The portal preserves that distinction at every layer.

Seven rules for interpretation

  • FRUS is a selected and edited documentary record, not the complete archive.
  • Subject mappings identify potentially relevant documents; they do not prove minerals are the central topic.
  • Volume dates and chapter headings are navigation context, not necessarily document-level dates or titles.
  • Absence from FRUS does not prove absence of U.S. interest or policy activity.
  • Historical actors often used terms other than “critical minerals.”
  • Modern categories should not be projected backward without explanation.
  • Historical resemblance is not a direct policy prescription.
Where to look beyond FRUS

NARA, presidential libraries, Defense, Commerce, USGS, aid-agency, intelligence, and congressional records may be necessary to reconstruct procurement, finance, industrial policy, or implementation.
